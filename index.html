Li Ding | 丁立

I'm a 4th-year Ph.D. candidate at UMass Amherst CICS, advised by Prof. Lee Spector. I also collaborate with Prof. Scott Niekum (UMass), Joel Lehman (CarperAI), and Prof. Jeff Clune (UBC). I interned at Google Research and Meta Reality Labs.

Previously, I was a full-time research engineer at MIT with Lex Fridman and Bryan Reimer, and concurrently a graduate student at MIT CSAIL. I did my master's at Univ. of Rochester with Prof. Chenliang Xu.

Research

My research focus is optimization algorithms and metaheuristics as they apply to:

  • Deep learning, meta-learning, architecture search, and knowledge distillation of foundation models and RL policies in single- and multi-objective scenarios.
  • Open-ended learning of large generative models and generally-capable agents.
  • Preference-based learning from human feedback and human-AI alignment.
  • Interdisciplinary machine learning for quantum computing and HCI.
