Curriculum Vitae

About

I'm a Ph.D. candidate in computer science at UMass Amherst, advised by Prof. Lee Spector. My primary research area is bio-inspired computational methods for optimization, deep learning, and meta-learning. I also have broader interests and published in disciplines including quantum machine learning and human-centered computing.

During Ph.D., I interned at Google Research and Meta Reality Labs. Previously, I was a research engineer at MIT with Dr. Lex Fridman and Dr. Bryan Reimer, and concurrently a part-time graduate student at MIT CSAIL. I received my master's degree in Data Science from the University of Rochester, where I got started on research with Prof. Chenliang Xu.
